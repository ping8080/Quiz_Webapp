Question No: 1

What is Gout?

Options:

  • 1.

    A degenerative disorder

  • 2.

    A vascular disorder

  • 3.

    A renal disorder

  • 4.

    A metabolic disorder

Solution:

    • Gout is a metabolic disorder caused by an imbalance in uric acid production and excretion. Hyperuricemia leads to the deposition of uric acid crystals in joints, resulting in inflammation and pain.

    Incorrect answer

    • A degenerative disorder: Gout is not degenerative like osteoarthritis, which involves the gradual deterioration of joint cartilage. Gout results from the accumulation of uric acid crystals, not wear and tear on joints.
    • A vascular disorder: Vascular disorders involve the blood vessels, such as arteriosclerosis or thrombosis. Gout primarily affects joints and soft tissues due to metabolic dysfunction rather than vascular issues.
    • A renal disorder: Although gout can be associated with kidney problems (like uric acid nephropathy or stones), it is not classified as a renal disorder. The primary issue lies in purine metabolism, leading to elevated uric acid levels.

    Reference:

    1. Joyce M Black, Jane Hokanson Hawks, Medical Surgical Nursing, Elsevier publisher, 7th edition, vol 2, pp. 606-608

    • An odd day is any day that remains after dividing the total number of days by 7.
    • 61 days / 7 = 8 weeks and 5 days

Question No: 52

What is the basic functional unit of kidney?

Options:

  • 1.

    Renal cortex

  • 2.

    Nephron

  • 3.

    Glomerulus

  • 4.

    Renal Medulla

Solution:

    • The nephron is the fundamental structural and functional unit of the kidney. It is responsible for filtering blood, removing waste, and balancing fluids, electrolytes, and acid-base levels in the body. Each nephron consists of a renal corpuscle (which includes the glomerulus and Bowman's capsule) and a renal tubule (which includes the proximal convoluted tubule, loop of Henle, distal convoluted tubule, and collecting duct). The nephron's primary function is filtration, reabsorption, and secretion, which contributes to urine formation.

    Incorrect answer:

    • Renal Cortex: The renal cortex is the outer portion of the kidney, which houses the renal corpuscles and the proximal and distal convoluted tubules of the nephrons. While it is essential for nephron function, it is not the basic functional unit itself. The nephron operates within the renal cortex, but the cortex is not a functional unit on its own.
    • Glomerulus: The glomerulus is a network of capillaries found in the renal corpuscle of each nephron, where blood filtration begins. Although the glomerulus plays a crucial role in the filtration process, it is not the entire functional unit of the kidney. The nephron includes the glomerulus along with other components involved in reabsorption and secretion.

    Renal Medulla: The renal medulla is the inner portion of the kidney, composed of renal pyramids, and is primarily involved in the concentration of urine. Like the renal cortex, it provides the environment for nephron function, but it is not the functional unit of the kidney. The nephron's primary components are spread across both the cortex and the medulla.
