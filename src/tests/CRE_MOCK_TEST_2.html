The nurse is checking the infants in the newborn nursery for hyperbilirubinemia. The nurse knows that the infants most likely to have hyperbilirubinemia are those who (Select all that apply.): 

  1. Are premature
  2. Are full term
  3. Have broken bones
  4. Have chromosomal disorders
  5. Have a large amount of bruising

Select the correct choices from the below-mentioned options:

Options:

  • 1.

    1, 5

  • 2.

    1, 3, 5

  • 3.

    1, 2

  • 4.

    2, 4

Solution:

    • Any condition that causes excessive bruising or affects the maturing of the liver will cause hyperbilirubinemia. In addition to those listed above, infants who are large for gestational age and those who suffer cold stress are more likely to have hyperbilirubinemia.
    • Are premature: Premature infants are at a higher risk for hyperbilirubinemia because their liver function may not be fully developed, leading to reduced ability to process and excrete bilirubin. Therefore, premature infants are more likely to develop jaundice.
    • Are full term: Full-term infants are less likely to experience hyperbilirubinemia compared to premature infants. However, they can still develop mild jaundice, especially if they are born with certain risk factors like blood type incompatibilities or bruising. But in general, full-term infants are less predisposed than premature infants.
    • Have broken bones: Broken bones can result in bruising and hematoma formation, which increases the breakdown of red blood cells, leading to the release of bilirubin. This can cause hyperbilirubinemia. Therefore, infants with broken bones are at increased risk for jaundice.
    • Have chromosomal disorders: Some chromosomal disorders (like Down syndrome) can increase the risk of jaundice due to factors like underdeveloped liver function. However, this is less directly related to hyperbilirubinemia than other factors like prematurity or bruising.
    • Have a large amount of bruising: Bruising or cephalohematoma (a collection of blood between the baby's skull and the periosteum) can cause increased red blood cell breakdown, leading to higher bilirubin levels and an increased risk of jaundice.
