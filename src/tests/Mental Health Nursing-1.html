Question No: 1

Which disease is dementia, associated with asymmetric atrophy of the frontal and temporal lobes?

Options:

  • 1. Vascular disease
  • 2. Pick`s disease
  • 3. Huntington disease
  • 4. Jakob disease

Solution:

    • Pick`s disease is dementia associated with asymmetric atrophy of the frontal and temporal lobes, it is a degenerative brain disease. This degeneration occurs due to deficiency of the proteins which are responsible for microtubules formations in neurons. Without microtubule’s brains, nerve tissue cannot survive and it goes under degeneration. This disease particularly affects the frontal and temporal lobes and results in a clinical picture similar to that of Alzheimer’s disease. Early signs include personality changes, loss of social skills and inhibitions, emotional blunting, and language abnormalities. Onset is most commonly 50 to 60 years of age; death occurs in 2 to 5 years.
    • Vascular disease: Vascular disease is related to blood vessels and in this, brain degeneration occurs in the cerebral cortex and subcortical structures resulting from the decreased blood supply.
    • Huntington disease: This is also a degenerative disorder but in this brain degeneration usually occurs in the area where body movement and gait are controlled such as basal ganglia.
    • Jakob’s disease: It is a fatal degenerative brain disorder. It causes rapid degeneration of the cerebral cortex or the outer layer of tissue surrounding the brain. Jakob’s disease is also known as Creutzfelft- Jakob’s disease.

Question No: 2

What is the other name for delusions of persecution?

Options:

  • 1. Infidelity
  • 2. Grandiose
  • 3. Passivity
  • 4. Paranoid delusion

Solution:

    • Paranoid delusion is the other name for delusions of persecution. Delusions of persecution refer to false beliefs or perceptions in which a person believes that they are being treated with malicious intent, hostility, or harassment-despite significant evidence to suggest otherwise. Imagine waking up and thinking that your next-door neighbor was plotting to kill you, or that your spouse had sprinkled some poison in your morning coffee. These are a couple of scenarios that may occur for individuals afflicted with persecutory delusions. Perceiving that someone is out to harm your reputation or sabotage your life are common themes of persecutory delusions. It is estimated that approximately 10% to 15% of the general population experience paranoid thoughts, and in some cases these thoughts result in persecutory delusions.
    • Infidelity: The action or state of being unfaithful to a spouse or sexual partner.
    • Grandiose: Impressive and imposing an appearance that is pretentious.
    • Passivity: Acceptance of what happened, without active response or resistance.

Question No: 6

What is the other name for fear of death?

Options:

  • 1. Claustrophobia
  • 2. Acrophobia
  • 3. Thanatophobia
  • 4. Gamophobia

Solution:

    • Thanatophobia is the other name for fear of death. In the Greek language, the word ‘Thanatos’ refers to death and ‘Phobos’ means fear. Thus, thanatophobia translates as the fear of death. Having some death anxiety is an entirely normal part of the human condition. However, for some people, thinking about their death or the process of dying can cause intense anxiety and fear. In their most extreme, these feelings can stop people from conducting daily activities or even leaving their homes. Their fears center on things that could result in death, such as contamination or dangerous objects or people.
    • Claustrophobia is a situational phobia triggered by an irrational and intense fear of tight or crowded spaces.
    • Acrophobia is an extreme or irrational fear or phobia of heights.
    • Gamophobia is the fear of commitment, though it can also be the excessive, persistent, uncontrollable, and irrational fear of marriage.

Question No: 7

Channeling of a strong and socially unacceptable drive or urge into a form that is acceptable to society is called as:

Options:

  • 1. Identification
  • 2. Sublimation
  • 3. Replacement
  • 4. Regression

Solution:

    • Channeling of a strong and socially unacceptable drive or urge into a form that is acceptable to society is called sublimation. Sublimation is a defense mechanism that allows us to act out unacceptable impulses by converting these behaviors into a more acceptable form. For example, a person experiencing extreme anger might take up kickboxing as a means of venting frustration.
    • Regression: When confronted by stressful events, people sometimes abandon coping strategies and revert to patterns of behavior used earlier in development. Anna Freud called this defense mechanism as regression, suggesting that people act out behaviors from the stage of psychosexual development in which they are fixated. For example, an individual fixated at an earlier developmental stage might cry or sulk upon hearing unpleasant news.
    • Identification: Identification is a psychological process whereby the individual assimilates an aspect, property, or attribute of the other and is transformed wholly or partially by the model that others provide.
    • Replacement: Not a defense mechanism.

Question No: 15

What are the immediate side effects of electroconvulsive therapy?

Options:

  • 1. Fractured and dislocated bones
  • 2. Permanent memory loss and brain damage
  • 3. Myocardial infarction and cardiac arrest
  • 4. Temporary memory loss and confusion

Solution:

    • After electroconvulsive therapy (ECT), the client may be mildly confused or briefly disoriented. The client experiences a headache and tiredness. The symptoms are just like those of anyone who has had a grand mal seizure. In addition, the client will have some short-term memory impairment. After therapy, the client may eat as soon as he or she is hungry and usually sleeps for a period. Headaches are treated symptomatically. Unilateral ECT results in less memory loss for the client, but more treatments may be needed to see sustained improvement. Bilateral ECT results in more rapid improvement but with increased short-term memory loss.
    • Fractures can sometimes occur in elderly patients with osteoporosis.
    • ECT does not cause any brain damage or permanent memory loss.
    • In patients with a history of heart disease, dysrhythmias and respiratory arrest may occur.

Question No: 27

The third phase of body’s adaptation to stress is:

Options:

  • 1. Reaction
  • 2. Exhaustion
  • 3. Resistance
  • 4. Rebound

Solution:

    • General Adaption Syndrome (GAS) has three different phases when exposed to stress. Exhaustion is the third phase. After an extended period of stress, the body goes into the final phase of GAS, i.e. the exhaustion phase. In this phase the person experiences tiredness, depression, anxiety, and feels unable to cope.
    • Reaction or alarm reaction phase is the first phase. In this the person responds to the stressor that is “fight or flight” response. Heart rate and blood pressure will increase.
    • Resistance is the second phase. The heart rate and blood pressure begin to return to normal. If the stressful situation comes to an end, during the resistance stage, the body will then return to normal, if the stressor remains, the body will stay in a state of alert, and stress hormones continue to be produced. This physical response can lead to a person struggling to concentrate and becoming irritable.
    • Rebound is not a phase of GAS hence it is a distractor.
