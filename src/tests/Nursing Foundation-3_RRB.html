Question No: 2

What is the name of the theory that proposes that patients have a unique set of needs that must be met in order to maintain health and well-being?

Options:

  • 1.

    Maslow’s Hierarchy of Needs

  • 2.

    Jean Watson’s Theory of Human Caring

  • 3.

    Virginia Henderson’s Need Theory

  • 4.

    Dorothea Orem’s Self-Care Deficit Theory

Solution:

  • Virginia Henderson's Need Theory:

    • Virginia Henderson's theory emphasizes that individuals have a unique set of basic human needs that must be met to achieve health and well-being. According to her theory, nurses assist patients in meeting these needs, particularly when patients are unable to do so independently.
    • She defined 14 basic needs that she believed are essential for individuals to maintain health and well-being, such as the need for oxygen, nutrition, elimination, movement, rest, and social interaction. 
    • The goal of nursing, according to Henderson, is to help patients achieve independence in meeting their needs as much as possible.
    • Option A) Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs is a theory of motivation, not a nursing theory.
    • Option B) Jean Watson's Theory of Human Caring is a theory of caring, not a theory of needs.
    • Option D) Dorothea Orem's Self-Care Deficit Theory is a theory of self-care, not a theory of needs.

Options:

  • 1. Sterilization
  • 2. Disinfection
  • 3. Sanitization
  • 4. Decontamination

Solution:

    • Disinfection refers to the process of using physical or chemical methods to reduce or eliminate harmful microorganisms (such as bacteria, viruses, or fungi) on a surface or object. However, disinfection does not necessarily eliminate all microorganisms (as sterilization does), but it significantly reduces their numbers to a level considered safe for health.
    • Option A) Sterilization is the process of eliminating all microorganisms on an object or surface.
    • Option C) Sanitization is the process of reducing the number of microorganisms on an object or surface to a safe level.
    • Option D) Decontamination is the process of removing contaminants, including microorganisms, from an object or surface.

    • Option d) Vitamin K: Vitamin K is used to treat bleeding caused by warfarin or other anticoagulants, but it is not an antidote for acetaminophen poisoning. It does not have any role in reversing the effects of acetaminophen overdose.
