Question No: 1

  • 1. The Speaker
  • 2. The President
  • 3. The Prime Minister

Solution:

    • In India, the Prime Minister is considered the head of government. While the President of India is the ceremonial head of state, the Prime Minister holds the executive power and is responsible for running the government.

    • The Vice- President is the second-highest constitutional office in India, who primarily acts as the Chairman of the Rajya Sabha (upper house) and steps in for the President, if needed.

Question No: 3

Which conventions are followed for spherical mirrors and lens?

Options:

  • 1.

    New Cartesian sign

  • 2.

    New Einstein sign

  • 3.

    New Huygens sign

  • 4.

    New Newton sign

Solution:

    • New Cartesian sign conventions are used for spherical mirrors and lens.
    • In the case of spherical mirrors and lenses, all signs are taken from the pole of the spherical mirror, origin of the lens. The principal axis of the mirror or lens is taken as the X-axis of the coordinate system and signs are given as per the new cartesian sign convention.

Question No: 4

The earth is at the maximum distance from the sun on:

Options:

  • 1.

    June 21st

  • 2.

    January 3rd

  • 3.

    July 4th

  • 4.

    August 3rd

Solution:

    • The Earth is farthest from the Sun during aphelion, which occurs around July 4th each year. Aphelion is the point in Earth’s orbit where it is at its maximum distance from the Sun.
    • The Earth is closest to the Sun during perihelion, which occurs around January 3rd.
    • 21 June is the summer solstice in the Northern Hemisphere, when the Sun is at its highest point in the sky.

Question No: 42

Murine typhus, that was recently seen in news, is caused by which agent?

Options:

  • 1. Fungus
  • 2. Bacteria
  • 3. Virus
  • 4. Protozoa

Solution:

    • Murine typhus is a disease caused by the bacteria Rickettsia typhi.
    • Murine typhus is spread to people through contact with infected fleas.
    • Fleas become infected when they bite infected animals, such as rats, cats, or opossums. Once infected, the flea remains infected for the remainder of its life.
    • When an infected flea bites a person or animal, the bite breaks the skin, causing a wound. Fleas poop when they feed. This poop (also called flea dirt) contains the bacteria, which can be rubbed into the bite wound or other wounds and cause an infection.
    • Symptoms of murine typhus include fever, chills, headache, and rash.

Question No: 46

What is the primary purpose of an Ossification test?

Options:

  • 1. To measure bone density
  • 2. To determine an individual’s age
  • 3. To detect blood group
  • 4. To assess level of diabetes

Solution:

    • Ossification refers to the process of bone formation.
    • An ossification test is often used to assess the maturity of bones, particularly in children and adolescents, to estimate their age based on the stage of bone development.
    • It analyzes bones to estimate age using x-rays of specific bones like the clavicle, sternum, and pelvis. These bones are chosen because they undergo significant changes as a person matures. Therefore, it is also used to assess skeletal maturity in children with growth disorders.
    • For example, the clavicle fuses at certain growth stages which can indicate age.
    • This is also used in forensic science and legal cases to establish age.
