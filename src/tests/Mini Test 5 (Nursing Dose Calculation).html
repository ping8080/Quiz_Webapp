Question No: 1

The doctor writes an order for a liquid oral medication. The order says to administer 15 mg by mouth every 4 hours as needed for sore throat. The available dose is 30 mg/3mL. How many mL should be administered to the patient?

Options:

  • 1. 0.5 mL/dose
  • 2. 3 mL/dose
  • 3. 1.5 mL/dose
  • 4. 6 mL/dose

Solution:

  • ·         STEP-I: The dosage calculation will be as per the formula:

    A= D/H*Q

     

    A= The amount of dose to be administered in mL.

    D= Desired dosage = 15 mg

    H= The dose in hand/available dose = 30 mg

    Q= Quantity in mL = 3 mL

     

    ·         STEP-II: Now, compute the values into the formula:

    A= 15/30*3

                = 1.5 mL/dose

    Hence, option c. is correct.
