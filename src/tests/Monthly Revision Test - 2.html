Question No: 1

Which of the following parameters is used to indicate the ability of a drug to produce the desired therapeutic effect relative to a toxic effect?

Options:

  • 1. Bioavailability
  • 2. Therapeutic Index
  • 3. Potency
  • 4. Efficacy

Solution:

  • ·    Therapeutic Index: It is calculated by dividing LD50 to ED50. LD50 is the minimum dose required to kill 50% of animals and hence is a measure of drug toxicity. ED50 is the minimum dose required to produce clinical effect in 50% of population and hence is a measure of drug potency (Lower dose required to produce effect, higher potency and vice versa).

    ·    Lithium is an example of a drug with a very low therapeutic index (TI), which requires frequent monitoring of the plasma level to achieve the balance between the desired effect and untoward toxicity.

    ·    Bioavailability of the drug is the fraction of the drug that reaches the blood stream unaltered.

    ·    Efficacy is the ability of the drug to generate maximal drug effect by acting on target. It is the most important parameter of drug clinically.

    ·    Potency of the drug is the amount of drug needed to produce a given response. The dose of drug required for an effect depends both on affinity and efficacy. Potency of two drugs actin on different targets can also be compared. If lesser dose is required for a particular effect, higher is the potency and vice versa.

Question No: 7

An unexpected effect of the drug, is known as:

Options:

  • 1. Side effect
  • 2. Adverse effect
  • 3. Toxic reaction
  • 4. Allergic reaction

Solution:

      • Adverse effects are harmful and unintended reactions that occur during normal drug administration. They are unexpected and can range from mild to severe. Adverse effects can occur regardless of whether the drug is taken correctly or not, and they often require intervention, such as discontinuing the drug or adjusting the dosage.

    Distractors:

      • a. Side effect:
        A side effect is an expected and predictable secondary effect that can occur alongside the desired therapeutic effect. These are often less harmful and usually not severe. Side effects are common, but they are not unexpected like adverse effects.
      • c. Toxic reaction:
        A toxic reaction refers to a drug's harmful effect caused by an excessive dose or prolonged use, leading to damage to organs or tissues. It is a specific type of adverse effect that results from overdose or drug accumulation.
      • d. Allergic reaction:
        An allergic reaction is a hypersensitivity reaction triggered by the immune system in response to a drug. While it can be unexpected, it is not synonymous with an adverse effect because it involves an immune-mediated response, not a harmful or unintended drug effect.
