Question No: 2

A patient present with the following vital signs:

  • Blood Pressure: 178/120
  • Pulse: 112
  • Respiratory Rate: 28
  • Oxygen Saturation: 98%
  • Temperature: 99.0°F

The patient reports a headache and blurred vision. Which of the following is the drug of choice for this condition?

Options:

  • 1.

    Metoprolol 100 mg orally (P.O.)

  • 2.

    Clonidine 0.3 mg orally (P.O.)

  • 3.

    Metoprolol 5 mg intravenously (IV)

  • 4.

    Paracetamol 650 mg orally (P.O.)

Solution:

  • A significant rise in blood pressure accompanied by clinical symptoms such as headache and blurred vision is indicative of a hypertensive emergency.

    Management of Hypertensive Emergency:

    The drugs of choice are intravenous calcium channel blockers (CCBs) such as Nicardipine and Clevidipine. If CCBs are not available or not an option, intravenous beta-blockers like Labetalol, Esmolol, or Metoprolol can be used as alternatives.

    Hypertensive Urgency:

    Hypertensive urgency is characterized by systolic blood pressure (SBP) ≥ 220 mmHg and/or diastolic blood pressure (DBP) ≥ 125 mmHg without evidence of end-organ damage.

    • The drug of choice for hypertensive urgency is oral Clonidine.
    • Other medications that may be used include Enalapril and Nifedipine.

    Explanation: Metoprolol, given intravenously, is the optimal choice in a hypertensive emergency because it rapidly reduces both blood pressure and heart rate. This addresses the hypertensive crisis effectively and promptly.

    • Correct: This is the most appropriate intervention for immediate treatment.

    Explanations:

    Option A: Metoprolol 100 mg orally (P.O.)

    • Explanation: Although oral metoprolol can lower blood pressure and heart rate, it takes approximately 30 minutes to act. In a hypertensive crisis, immediate action is necessary, and IV medication is preferred for rapid effect.
    • Incorrect: Oral administration is not appropriate for a hypertensive emergency due to delayed onset.

    Option B: Clonidine 0.3 mg orally (P.O.)

    • Explanation: Clonidine is effective for managing hypertensive emergencies but does not specifically address the elevated heart rate in this case. Additionally, oral clonidine would take longer to act compared to IV antihypertensives.
    • Incorrect: Clonidine is not the most suitable choice due to the patient's tachycardia and the delayed onset of the oral route.

    Option D: Paracetamol 650 mg orally (P.O.)

    • Explanation: The patient’s headache is a symptom of elevated blood pressure, which will improve as the hypertension is treated. Paracetamol is unnecessary for addressing the underlying cause of the headache.
    • Incorrect: Treating the headache symptomatically without addressing the hypertensive crisis is inadequate care.

Question No: 8

Inhaled nitric oxide (iNO) is used for?

Options:

  • 1.

    Persistent pulmonary hypertension of newborn

  • 2.

    Unstable angina

  • 3.

    Obstructive sleep apnea

  • 4.

    Erectile dysfunction

Solution:

    • Inhaled nitric oxide(iNO) is used for persistent pulmonary hypertension in newborns. (Option A)
    • Inhaled NO selectively dilates the pulmonary vasculature, hence used in diseases associated with increased pulmonary vascular resistance. Inhaled NO is an FDA-approved drug for persistent pulmonary hypertension in the newborn.
    • Inhaled NO can also be used during cardiac catheterization to evaluate the pulmonary vasodilating capacity of patients with heart failure and infants with congenital heart disease. Another use of inhaled NO is to determine the diffusion capacity (DL) across the alveolar-capillary membrane. It is more effective than CO2 because of its greater affinity for hemoglobin and its higher water solubility at body temperature.
    • NO is produced from the nasal passages and the lungs of normal human subjects and can be detected in exhaled gas. The measurement of fractional exhaled NO is a non-invasive marker for airway inflammation in the assessment of respiratory tract diseases, including asthma, respiratory tract infection, and chronic lung disease.
    • Inhaled NO is administered at low concentrations (0.1-50 ppm) since high concentrations of 50 to 100 ppm can cause significant methemoglobinemia that can cause death.

    Other options:

    • Option B: Unstable angina is treated by anti-platelet drugs such as aspirin and clopidogrel, and anticoagulants such as heparin.
    • Option C: Obstructive sleep apnoea is mainly treated by using a BiPAP machine during sleep and CNS stimulants such as armodafinil for daytime sleepiness associated with the condition.
    • Option D: Erectile dysfunction is treated by PDE5 inhibitors such as sildenafil, tadalafil, etc.

Question No: 10

Which of the following drugs can be used in diabetic neuropathy, post herpetic neuralgia and spinal cord injury?

Options:

  • 1. Amitriptyline
  • 2. Phenytoin
  • 3. Carbamazepine
  • 4. Pregabalin

Solution:

    • Pregabalin can be used for diabetic neuropathy, post herpetic neuralgia and spinal cord injury. Although amitriptyline can also be used for these conditions, pregabalin is found to be recommended over amitriptyline considering safety and efficacy and hence is the best option.
    • Pregabalin is an anti-epileptic drug. Its analgesic effect is mediated by binding to α2δ, a protein that is a subunit of voltage-gated calcium channels and causes a decrease in glutamate release at excitatory synapses. Decreased neurotransmitter release is associated with reduced neuronal hyperexcitability. It is used in the management of neuropathic pain associated with diabetic peripheral neuropathy, spinal cord injury or post herpetic neuralgia.
    • Currently, only duloxetine and pregabalin are approved by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration and the European Medicines Agency to treat painful diabetic neuropathy. Diabetic Neuropathy is the most common cause of peripheral neuropathy in developed countries. Risk factors include long-standing, poorly controlled DM and the presence of retinopathy and nephropathy.
    • Side effects of pregabalin include sedation and peripheral edema.

    Other options:

    • Option A: Tricyclic antidepressants (TCAs) such as amitriptyline are used in the treatment of several pain conditions such as diabetic neuropathy, post herpetic neuralgia and following spinal cord injury. Their mechanism of action is inhibition of norepinephrine and serotonin reuptake. Although both pregabalin and amitriptyline are among the first line drugs for neuropathic pain, amitriptyline would be the next best option in this question, owing to its side effect profile and good yet controversial efficacy in pain control. It is a non FDA approved drug for diabetic neuropathy (off label indication). Side effects associated with amitriptyline include anticholinergic side effects (dry mouth, tachycardia, hyperthermia), cardiac effects (arrhythmias) and sedation.
    • Option B: Phenytoin is an antiepileptic drug that is not used in the treatment of pain.
    • Option C: Carbamazepine is an iminostilbine derivative. It blocks voltage-activated sodium channels, slowing their rate of recovery and limiting the repetitive firing of action potentials. It is used in the treatment of generalized, focal-to-bilateral and idiopathic tonic-clonic seizures, focal seizures, neuropathic pain associated with trigeminal and glossopharyngeal neuralgias.

Question No: 13

A 23-year-old primiparous woman presented with features suggestive of thyrotoxicosis, at 7 weeks of gestation. Which of the following is the drug of choice for the treatment of her condition?

Options:

  • 1.

    Methimazole

  • 2.

    Carbimazole

  • 3.

    Propylthiouracil

  • 4.

    Radioactive iodine

Solution:

    • The drug of choice for the treatment of thyrotoxicosis during the first trimester of pregnancy is propylthiouracil (PTU). It is because it has lower teratogenic effects than carbimazole or methimazole.
    • Carbimazole and methimazole are associated with teratogenic effects such as:
      • Aplasia cutis congenita
      • Choanal atresia
      • Tracheoesophageal fistula
      • Fetal goiter
    • However, propylthiouracil use is limited to the first trimester due to the risk of hepatotoxicity. It is replaced with carbimazole or methimazole in the 2nd and 3rd trimesters of the pregnancy.
    • The lowest effective antithyroid drug dose should be used throughout gestation. This is to maintain the maternal serum-free T4 level in the upper limit of the non-pregnant normal reference range.
    • Radioiodine therapy is contraindicated during pregnancy and lactation.
    • Drug of choice for hyperthyroidism/thyrotoxicosis in pregnancy
    • In the 1st trimester: Propylthiouracil
    • In the 2nd and 3rd trimester: Methimazole

An elderly patient is brought to the emergency room in a drowsy state after taking an overdose of his sleeping pills. His medical history includes insomnia for which he was on alprazolam. Which of the following medication will you administer to this patient?

Options:

  • 1.

    Naloxone

  • 2.

    Flumazenil

  • 3.

    Naltrexone

  • 4.

    Nalmefene

Solution:

    • The patient is presenting with benzodiazepine (alprazolam) Flumazenil is the drug of choice for this patient.       
    • Flumazenil is a competitive antagonist at the benzodiazepine binding site of the GABAA It antagonizes both the agonist (benzodiazepines) and inverse-agonist (beta-carbolines) of this receptor.     
    • It is primarily indicated for the management of benzodiazepine overdose and the reversal of benzodiazepine anesthesia.    

    Other options:   

    • Option A: Naloxone is an opioid antagonist used in the management of opioid overdose.     
    • Option C: Naltrexone, another opioid antagonist, is used in the management of alcohol dependence, prevention of relapse in opioid deaddiction after detoxification, and weight management. It might cause liver failure.
    • Option D: Nalmefene is a pure opioid antagonist with higher potency than naloxone. It is used for the treatment of opioid toxicity and substance abuse.

    Pure opioid antagonists

    • Naloxone: Short-acting opioid antagonist with duration of action of 1-2 hours. Used in opioid overdose and respiratory depression in newborns due to perinatal opioid use.
    • Naltrexone: Long-acting opioid antagonist with effects lasting till 48 hours. Used for prevention of relapse in opioid addiction treatment. It is associated with hepatotoxicity. It does not suppress craving in opioid addiction but suppresses craving in chronic alcoholics.
    • Nalmefene: Long-acting opioid antagonist with a half-life of 8-10 hours administered intravenously. Used in opioid overdose like naloxone.

Question No: 17

The drug of choice in anaphylactic shock is:

Options:

  • 1.

    Adrenaline 0.5 mg 1/1000 IM

  • 2.

    Adrenaline 1 mg 1/10000 IV

  • 3.

    Atropine 3 mg

  • 4.

    Adenosine 6 mg followed by 12 mg IV

Solution:

    • Adrenaline 0.5 mg 1/1000 by intramuscular route (IM) is the drug of choice for treatment of anaphylactic shock. Hence it is the answer here.
    • In case the patient develops life threatening condition, adrenaline 0.25 mg IV is given.

    Note: This is a controversial question. The following inference from journal may help to clarify the concept.

    • As vasodilatation is the main pathological change early in anaphylaxis, this enables the subcutaneous or intramuscular absorption of adrenaline to be rapid and effective.
    • Thus, when the disease is treated early and is progressing slowly, or venous access is difficult or the patient is unmonitored, intramuscular adrenaline has advantages in terms of safety and is usually effective.
    • Later, when intravascular volume is depleted and shock occurs with a life threatening situation, or there is severe dyspnoea or airway compromise, the intravenous route is necessary to achieve optimal absorption.
