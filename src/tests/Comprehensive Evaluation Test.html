This ring is a classic sign of Wilson's disease, a genetic disorder that leads to copper accumulation in the liver, brain, and other tissues.

    Review of the other options:

    • Option A. Black discoloration at the outer margin of the cornea because of the deposition of copper in Descemet's membrane.
      • Incorrect: The Kayser-Fleischer ring is not typically black in color. It is more commonly described as brown or golden. Also, it is located at the outer margin of the cornea, but the color is not black.
    • Option B. Brown discoloration at the inner margin of the cornea because of the deposition of copper in Descemet's membrane.

      • Incorrect: The Kayser-Fleischer ring is not located in the retina, but in the cornea. Copper deposition in the retina is associated with other conditions, but the Kayser-Fleischer ring is specific to the cornea.

    Thus, the best description of the Kayser-Fleischer ring is given in option D: It appears as a brown discoloration at the outer margin of the cornea due to copper deposition in Descemet's membrane.
