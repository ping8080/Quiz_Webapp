Solution:

    • The conjunctiva is a thin, transparent mucous membrane that lines the inside of the eyelids and covers the sclera (the white part of the eye). It plays a crucial role in protecting the eye from debris and infections and helps maintain lubrication by producing mucus and tears.

    Summary: The conjunctiva is a protective, transparent mucous membrane that lines the inner surface of the eyelids and partially covers the sclera. It is distinct from the canthi, optic disc, or tear-drainage system, which are unrelated to its structure or function.

    Answer d. (1, 2, 3, and 4) includes all relevant complications: Because the ear contains both the auditory and vestibular systems, trauma can affect multiple structures, making a comprehensive evaluation essential.
