Question No: 1

Which of the following is the most appropriate indication for a cesarean section?

Options:

  • 1. Breech presentation at 38 weeks
  • 2. Mild preeclampsia without fetal compromise
  • 3. Prolonged latent phase of labor
  • 4. Spontaneous rupture of membranes at 39 weeks with no labor

Solution:

    • A cesarean section is commonly indicated for breech presentation at term due to the risk of cord prolapse, head entrapment, and birth injuries in a vaginal delivery.
    • Option B) Mild preeclampsia without fetal compromise:Mild preeclampsia can often be managed with careful monitoring and vaginal delivery, especially if there are no signs of fetal distress or maternal complications.
    • Option C) Prolonged latent phase of labor: This is typically not an indication for cesarean delivery; it can often be managed with expectant management or the use of labor augmentation.
    • Option D) Spontaneous rupture of membranes at 39 weeks with no labor: This is not an indication for cesarean unless there is infection or fetal distress. Many women with ruptured membranes at term can be managed expectantly or with induction of labor.
