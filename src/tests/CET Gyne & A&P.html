Question No: 1

Dysmenorrhea is:

Options:

  • 1.

    Painful menstruation

  • 2.

    Increased frequency of menstruation

  • 3.

    Increased menses during the reproductive period

  • 4.

    The absence of menses during the reproductive period

Solution:

    • Dysmenorrhea means painful menstruation of sufficient magnitude so as to incapacitate day to day activities.

    There are two types:

    • Primary dysmenorrhea: Pain occurs without underlying reproductive disorders, typically starting within a few years of the onset of menstruation.
    • Secondary dysmenorrhea: Pain caused by an underlying condition, such as endometriosis or fibroids.

    The other options refer to different menstrual conditions:

    • Option B. Increased frequency of menstruation: This is called polymenorrhea.
    • Option C. Increased menses during the reproductive period: This is known as menorrhagia (heavy menstrual bleeding).
    • Option D. The absence of menses during the reproductive period: This is referred to as amenorrhea.
