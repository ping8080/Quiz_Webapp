Question No: 5

Which of the following tests requires both a blood sample and a 24-hour urine collection? (Renal Function Tests)

Options:

  • 1.

    Urine cytology

  • 2.

    Creatinine clearance

  • 3.

    Bicarbonate

  • 4.

    BUN

Solution:

    • The creatinine clearance test requires a 24-hour urine collection in addition to a blood sample. These samples are used to assess the rate at which the kidneys are clearing creatinine from the bloodstream, providing insight into kidney function.
    • Urine cytology: Urine cytology relies solely on a urine sample to analyze cells within the urinary tract, often used to detect infections, abnormal cells, or potential malignancies.
    • Bicarbonate: Bicarbonate levels are usually measured using a blood sample, though they may also be assessed in a urine sample. This test evaluates the body’s acid-base balance but does not require a 24-hour urine collection.
    • BUN (Blood Urea Nitrogen): BUN is measured with a blood sample to determine the amount of urea nitrogen in the bloodstream, providing an indication of kidney health without needing a urine sample.

While treating a patient with protein-calorie malnutrition, which laboratory result indicates improvement in their condition? (Nutritional disorders)

Options:

  • 1.

    Reduced lymphocyte count

  • 2.

    Elevated serum potassium

  • 3.

    Increased serum transferrin

  • 4.

    Lowered serum pre-albumin

Solution:

    • Serum transferrin is a protein produced by the liver that transports iron. It is sensitive to protein levels and decreases during protein deficiency. An increase in transferrin suggests a positive nitrogen balance and recovery of protein synthesis, indicating improvement.
    • Option a) Reduced lymphocyte count (Incorrect):
      A decrease in lymphocyte count often indicates protein depletion and compromised immune function. Improvement in protein-calorie malnutrition is associated with an increase in lymphocyte count, not a reduction.

    • Option d) Lowered serum pre-albumin (Incorrect):
      Decreased serum pre-albumin levels are a marker of protein depletion. Improvement would be shown by an increase in pre-albumin levels, reflecting better protein and nutritional status.

    His blood glucose levels have been consistently normal.

  • 2.

    He has had high blood glucose levels over the past 2-3 months.

  • 3.

    His blood glucose levels are unstable.

  • 4.

    He is at risk for hypoglycemia.

Solution:

    • HbA1c is a test that measures the average level of blood glucose over the past 2-3 months. An HbA1c of 8.5% is above the recommended target for most individuals with diabetes (typically <7%).

    • There is no direct indication of hypoglycemia (which is low blood glucose) from an HbA1c value of 8.5%; this value suggests chronic high blood glucose levels instead.

    • 4.

    Solution:

      • High-Density Lipoprotein (HDL) plays a critical role in lipid metabolism through a process known as reverse cholesterol transport.
