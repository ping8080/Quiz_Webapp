Question No: 1

The primary method for controlling the spread of malaria in endemic regions is:

Options:

  • 1.

    Use of antibiotics 

  • 2.

    Antiviral vaccination

  • 3.

    Vector control through insecticide-treated nets and indoor spraying

  • 4.

    Quarantine of infected individuals 

Solution:

  • Malaria is transmitted through Anopheles mosquitoes, and controlling the vector through methods like insecticide-treated nets and indoor spraying is the most effective strategy for malaria control.

    • Option A) Antibiotics are not effective against malaria, as it is caused by a protozoan parasite (Plasmodium).
    • Option B) There is no antiviral vaccine for malaria, as it is a parasitic disease.
    • Option D) Quarantine of infected individuals does not control the spread of malaria, as it is primarily transmitted by mosquitoes, not directly from person to person.

Question No: 2

Which of the following is the most accurate method for diagnosing active tuberculosis (TB)?

Options:

  • 1.

    Chest X-ray

  • 2.

    Sputum smear microscopy for acid-fast bacilli

  • 3.

    Tuberculin skin test (TST) 

  • 4.

    Polymerase chain reaction (PCR) for Mycobacterium tuberculosis

Solution:

  • PCR is the most accurate method for diagnosing active TB, as it detects the genetic material of Mycobacterium tuberculosis with high specificity and sensitivity.

    • Option A) Chest X-rays are useful for detecting lung abnormalities but cannot definitively diagnose active TB.   
    • Option B) Sputum smear microscopy is commonly used but has lower sensitivity, especially in people with low bacterial load or extrapulmonary TB.
    • Option C) Tuberculin skin test (TST) is a screening test and cannot differentiate between latent and active TB infections.
