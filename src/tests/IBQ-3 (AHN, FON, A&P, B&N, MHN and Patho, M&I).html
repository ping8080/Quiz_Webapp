Which of the following abnormalities is associated with this condition?

Options:

  • 1.

    Glutamic acid in 6th position of beta chain is replaced by histidine

  • 2.

  • 3.

    Glutamic acid in 6th position of beta chain is replaced by valine

  • 4.

    Glutamic acid in 6th position of alpha chain is replaced by tryptophan

Solution:

    • The given image shows sickled red blood cells. The primary defect in HbS is the amino acid glutamic acid in the 6th position of the beta chain of hemoglobin is replaced by valine. This mutation is responsible for sickle cell anemia, where a substitution of glutamic acid with valine in the 6th position of the beta-globin chain of hemoglobin (HbS) causes the hemoglobin molecules to aggregate, leading to the characteristic sickling of red blood cells.
    • Glutamic acid in 6th position of beta chain is replaced by histidine: This substitution does not cause sickle cell disease. Histidine is a polar amino acid, and replacing glutamic acid (also polar) with histidine at position 6 of the beta chain would not result in the hydrophobic interactions that are characteristic of sickle cell hemoglobin (HbS). This mutation doesn't cause the sickling phenomenon.
    • Glutamic acid in 6th position of alpha chain is replaced by proline: Sickle cell disease is caused by a mutation in the beta chain, not the alpha chain. The alpha chain does not undergo the same mutation as the beta chain in sickle cell disease. A substitution of glutamic acid with proline at position 6 of the alpha chain is not associated with the sickling of red blood cells.   
    • Glutamic acid in 6th position of alpha chain is replaced by tryptophan: Similar to option b, this mutation affects the alpha chain, which is not the cause of sickle cell anemia. Sickle cell anemia is specifically caused by a mutation in the beta-globin chain, not the alpha-globin chain. Tryptophan is an aromatic amino acid, and this substitution wouldn't create the hydrophobic interactions leading to sickling in the way that valine does in the beta chain.

    Prevent collection of mucous

  • 2.

    Promote oxygenation

  • 3.

    Prevent atelectasis

  • 4.

    Prevent hypercarbia

Solution:

  • The purpose of incentive spirometry in a post-operative patient is to prevent pulmonary atelectasis.      

    • Atelectasis, especially of the lower lobes, is the most common respiratory complication in a post-operative patient and can be prevented by sitting the patient or inclining the bed, early mobilization, deep breathing exercises with incentive spirometry, and chest physiotherapy. Patients who are not fully awake may require non-invasive mechanical ventilation with Correct manner of performing incentive spirometry is by taking sequential deep breaths so that all the balls or marker in the tube rise to the top, holding the breath for 5-6 seconds, and then exhaling.
    • The primary pathophysiology is inadequate expansion of the alveoli leading to their collapse. The factors predisposing to atelectasis are general anesthesia, upper abdominal and thoracic surgery. In the post-operative period, inadequate pain control may lead to poor inspiratory effort and reduced diaphragm contraction in the patient's attempt to avoid experiencing pain, may also contribute to the development of atelectasis. On the other hand, overuse of opioid analgesics or sedatives may also favor development of atelectasis due to respiratory depression causing collection of secretions. Other causes include lack of mobilization (bed-bound patients) or positional change.
    • Patients present with tachypnea and decreased oxygen saturation and on examination, the breath sounds are bronchial in nature and reduced in intensity. Chest X-ray may show diminished lung volume, linear opacities, and elevation of the diaphragm on the affected side.

    Moist heat causes coagulation and denaturation of proteins, while dry heat causes oxidation and charring of proteins. Hence not preferred for egg-containing culture media like the Lowenstein-Jensen medium.

Question No: 50

A newly pregnant woman from a rural area registered in the scheme shown below. Which of the following benefits would she receive under this scheme?

Options:

  • 1.

    A cash incentive of 5000 rupees

  • 2.

    At least 4 antenatal checkups and 6 homebased newborn care visits

  • 3.

    Free checkup on 9th of every month

  • 4.

    A cash incentive of 1400 rupees

Solution:

    • The above image shows the logo of Pradhan Mantri Surakshit Matritva Abhiyan (PMSMA). Under this scheme, all pregnant women receive a free checkup on the 9th of every month.  
    • PMSMA aims to improve the quality and coverage of antenatal care, diagnostics, and counseling services. This is undertaken as part of the reproductive maternal neonatal child and adolescent health (RMNCH+A) strategy. Under PMSMA, a pregnant woman receives at least 1 check-up in her second or third trimester by a doctor in the PMSMA clinic, on the 9th of every month.  
    • After routine examination, pregnant women are categorized according to the risk factors, and different colored stickers are given as follows:
    • Green sticker: For women with no detected risk factor
    • Red sticker: For women with detected risk factor

    Other options:

    • Option A: A cash incentive of 5000 rupees is provided to all pregnant women and lactating mothers under Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana (PMMVY) for the first living child of the family. This is subject to fulfillment of specific conditions.

    • Option B: At least 4 antenatal checkups and 6 home-based newborn care visits are provided under Surakshit Matritva Aashwasan (SUMAN). It is a new initiative for women and newborns where assured, dignified, respectful, and quality healthcare is provided free of cost. It aims to end all preventable maternal and newborn deaths.
    • Option D: A cash incentive of 1400 rupees is provided to all pregnant women who underwent an institutional delivery in low-performing states under Janani Suraksha Yojana (JSY). This scheme is launched under NRHM (National Rural Health Mission) to promote institutional delivery.
