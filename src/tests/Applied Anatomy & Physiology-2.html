Options:

    The islets of Langerhans (pancreatic islets)

  • 2.

    The thyroid gland

  • 3.

    The acini cells of the pancreas

  • 4.

    The parathyroid glands

Solution:

  • The acini cells produce digestive enzymes.      

    • The islets of Langerhans (pancreatic islets): These are clusters of cells in the pancreas that function as part of the endocrine system. They secrete hormones like insulin and glucagon to regulate blood sugar levels.

Question No: 8

Primary receptor cells of hearing:

Options:

  • 1.

    Tunnel of Corti

  • 2.

    Supporting cell

  • 3.

    Hair cells

  • 4.

    Tectorial membrane

Solution:

    • Hair cells are primary receptor cell that transduce sound energy into electrical energy. Two groups of hair cells lie on the basilar membrane-inner hair cells and outer hair cells these are responsible for detecting the presence of sound.
    • First order neurons are the bipolar cells of the spiral ganglion. Dendrites of the bipolar cells constitute the afferent fibers innervating the hair cells.
    • Tunnel of Corti: The Tunnel of Corti is a structure within the cochlea that separates two rows of hair cells, but it is not the receptor itself. It is part of the cochlear anatomy, but it does not detect sound.
    • Supporting cell: Supporting cells are found in the cochlea, but they do not act as primary receptor cells for hearing. Their function is more related to providing structural support, and in some cases, they can play a role in regenerating hair cells, but they are not responsible for detecting sound.
    • Tectorial membrane: The tectorial membrane is a gel-like structure in the cochlea that interacts with the stereocilia of the hair cells. While it plays a crucial role in sound transduction, it is not a receptor itself.

Question No: 11

An immovable joint is called:

Options:

  • 1. Synarthrosis
  • 2. Polyarthritis
  • 3. Amphiarthrosis
  • 4. Diarthrosis

Solution:

    • An immobile or nearly immobile joint is called a synarthrosis. Examples include sutures, the fibrous joints between the bones of the skull that surround and protect the brain, and the manubriosternal joint and the cartilaginous joint that unite the manubrium and body of the sternum for protection of the heart.
    • Polyarthritis is present when four or more joints in the body become painful and inflamed. It can be caused by viral infection. Polyarthritis has a number of symptoms similar to rheumatoid arthritis, including pain, swelling, and stiffness in affected joints.
    • An amphiarthrosis is a joint that has limited mobility. Example is pubic symphysis of the pelvis. This is a cartilaginous joint in which the pubic regions of the right and left hip bones are strongly anchored to each other by fibrocartilage. This joint normally has very little mobility. The strength of the pubic symphysis is important in conferring weight-bearing stability to the pelvis.
    • A freely mobile joint is classified as a diarthrosis. These types of joints include all synovial joints of the body, which provide the majority of body movements. Synovial joints are the ball-and-socket joint (shoulder joint), hinge joint (knee).
