Question No: 1

Which statement correctly defines the glomerular filtration rate (GFR)?

Options:

  • 1. The main role of GFR is to remove nitrogenous waste products from the body.
  • 2. Reduced glomerular permeability results in the excretion of proteins in the urine.
  • 3. GFR is primarily influenced by sufficient blood flow and hydrostatic pressure.
  • 4. GFR decreases when prostaglandins lead to vasodilation and increased renal blood flow.

Solution:

  • GFR depends on an adequate supply of blood to the glomeruli and the hydrostatic pressure needed to drive filtration. Changes in these factors can significantly impact GFR and overall kidney function.
  • The main role of GFR is to remove nitrogenous waste products from the body.

    Explanation:

    This is incorrect. While the kidneys do excrete nitrogenous waste as part of their function, GFR specifically refers to the rate at which blood is filtered by the glomeruli. It does not directly describe the excretion process.
  • Reduced glomerular permeability results in the excretion of proteins in the urine.

    Explanation:

    This statement is incorrect. Loss of proteins into the urine, known as proteinuria, typically occurs due to increased glomerular permeability, not decreased permeability.
  • GFR decreases when prostaglandins lead to vasodilation and increased renal blood flow.

    Explanation:

    This is incorrect. Prostaglandins generally act to increase renal blood flow through vasodilation, which helps maintain or increase GFR rather than decrease it.
