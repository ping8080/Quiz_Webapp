Question No: 1

Which traditional educational philosophy emphasizes learning through experience, problem-solving, and adaptability, making it highly relevant for nursing simulation-based training?

Options:

  • 1.

    Idealism

  • 2.

    Naturalism

  • 3.

    Pragmatism 

  • 4.

    Realism

Solution:

    • Pragmatism focuses on learning through experience, problem-solving, and practical application of knowledge. This aligns with nursing education methods like simulation-based learning, case studies, and clinical rotations, where nurses develop critical thinking and adaptability to real-world scenarios.
    • John Dewey, a key proponent of pragmatism, emphasized experiential learning, which is crucial in nursing practice.

    Distractor Analysis:

    • Idealism → Focuses on theoretical knowledge and absolute truths, with less emphasis on hands-on learning. Nursing requires practical skills, making this approach less effective for clinical training.
    • Naturalism → Stresses learning in a natural environment, but lacks the structured problem-solving and adaptability components needed in nursing education.     
    • Realism → Focuses on objective facts and scientific laws, which are important in nursing, but lacks the problem-solving approach of pragmatism.  

    Traditional Educational Philosophies:

    • Naturalism → Learning occurs naturally, focusing on experience, observation, and environmental adaptation; aligns with self-directed and experiential nursing education.
    • Idealism → Emphasizes abstract thinking, moral values, and universal truths; supports ethics, leadership, and holistic nursing education.
    • Pragmatism → Learning is practical, problem-solving, and experience-based, promoting simulation, case studies, and evidence-based practice in nursing.  
    • Realism → Focuses on objective facts, scientific knowledge, and logical reasoning, forming the basis of biomedical sciences and evidence-based nursing care.

Question No: 12

Which of the following means translating the message into verbal and nonverbal symbols to communicate to the receiver?

Options:

  • 1.

    Encoding

  • 2.

    Decoding

  • 3.

    Channel

  • 4.

    Feedback

Solution:

    • Encoding refers to the process of translating information into a message using verbal and nonverbal symbols (e.g., words, gestures, body language). This is the first step in the communication process, where the sender creates a message that will be transmitted to the receiver. In encoding, the sender must convert their thoughts or ideas into an appropriate form that can be easily understood by the receiver.
    • Decoding: Decoding is the opposite process, where the receiver interprets the message. It involves understanding, interpreting, and evaluating the message. Decoding occurs after encoding and transmission, as the receiver's job is to interpret what has been communicated.
    • Channel: The channel refers to the medium through which the message is communicated, such as face-to-face communication, telephone, email, etc. It is the pathway through which the message flows, not the process of translating the message itself.
    • Feedback: Feedback is the response from the receiver to the sender. It provides confirmation that the message has been understood or clarifies the need for further communication. Feedback may be verbal (e.g., speaking) or nonverbal (e.g., gestures, facial expressions), but it is not involved in the creation of the original message.

    Key Points:

    • Encoding = Message creation using symbols.
    • Decoding = Message interpretation by the receiver.
    • Channel = Medium used for communication.
    • Feedback = Response from the receiver to the sender.

Options:

  • 1.

    Remembering 

  • 2.

    Understanding

  • 3.

    Creating

  • 4.

    Analyzing  

Solution:

    • Creating is a new addition to the revised Bloom's Taxonomy. It was added to emphasize the importance of generating new ideas, products, or ways of understanding. This level involves tasks such as designing, constructing, planning, and producing original work.

    Distractors:

    • Option A) Remembering: Remembering is one of the original six levels of Bloom's Taxonomy. It involves recalling facts, terms, basic concepts, or answers.

    Summary: The revised Bloom's Taxonomy includes Creating as a new level to highlight the importance of innovation and creativity in the learning process. This update reflects the evolving nature of education and the need to foster higher-order thinking skills.

Options:

  • 1.

    To minimize workload 

  • 2.

    To enhance efficiency

  • 3.

    To collect information

  • 4.

    To collect reliable responses from experts

Solution:

    • To collect information: Collecting information is a broader goal that many methods can achieve. The Delphi technique is more specific in its approach, targeting the collection of reliable and validated responses from experts, rather than just gathering information in general.

Question No: 20

The most appropriate method for teaching psychomotor skills in nursing education is: 

Options:

  • 1.

    Lecture method

  • 2.

    Group discussion

  • 3.

    Demonstration method

  • 4.

    Role-playing

Solution:

  • The demonstration method is the most effective approach for teaching psychomotor skills in nursing education because:

    • Learning by observation and practice – Students learn best when they can see a skill performed correctly before attempting it themselves.
    • Step-by-step guidance – It allows sequential learning, ensuring clarity in skill execution.
    • Immediate feedback – Learners receive real-time corrections to refine their skills and improve performance.
    • Enhanced retention – The combination of visual, auditory, and kinaesthetic learning reinforces long-term memory.
    • Skill proficiency and safety – Essential nursing skills like IV insertion, wound dressing, CPR, and catheterization require precision, which is best achieved through hands-on demonstration.

    Explanation of Distractors:

    Option a) Lecture Method – Incorrect

    • The lecture method is effective for teaching theoretical knowledge but not suitable for psychomotor skill development because skills require hands-on practice, not passive listening.
    • Example: A nurse cannot learn how to insert an IV cannula just by listening to a lecture.

    Option b) Group Discussion – Incorrect

    • Group discussions are useful for conceptual understanding, problem-solving, and critical thinking but not for skill-based learning.
    • Example: Discussing CPR steps in a group does not ensure that a student can perform chest compressions correctly.

    Option d) Role-Playing – Incorrect

    • Role-playing is effective for teaching communication, leadership, and ethical decision-making, but it does not provide structured step-by-step learning for psychomotor skills.
    • Example: Practicing patient interaction through role-playing cannot replace the hands-on learning required for wound dressing or tracheostomy care.

    Conclusion: Since psychomotor skills involve motor coordination, precision, and stepwise execution, the demonstration method remains the gold standard in nursing education. The other methods may complement learning but are not the most appropriate for skill mastery.

    Didactic lectures

  • 4.

    • Dictation is used for note-taking and language learning, not critical thinking.
