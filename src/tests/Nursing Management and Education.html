    • Legitimate Power: It is the power of formal position or office held in the organization’s hierarchy of authority.

Question No: 3

Transferring of responsibility to subordinates on behalf of the manager is called as: 

Options:

  • 1. Decision making
  • 2. Coordination
  • 3. Leadership
  • 4. Delegation

Solution:

    • Transferring responsibility to subordinates on behalf of the manager is called delegation. Delegation is the process of assigning part or all of one person’s responsibility to another person or persons. Delegating is an effective management competency by which nurse managers get the work done through the employees. The purpose of delegation is efficiency; no one person can do all the work that must be done; therefore, some work must be passed on, or delegated to others. However, it must be remembered that, even when an activity is delegated to someone else, the ultimate responsibility for that activity still belongs to the nurse leader (i.e. the person who delegated the activity). Nurse managers need to be able to delegate some of their duties, tasks, and responsibilities as a solution to overwork, which leads to stress, anger, and aggression. As nurse managers learn to accept the principle of delegation, they become more productive and come to enjoy relationships with the staff.

Question No: 10

Options:

  • 1. Norming
  • 2. Forming
  • 3. Storming
  • 4. Performing

    • Performing is a stage of a fully functional group where members see themselves as a group and get involved in the task. Each person makes a contribution and the authority figure is also seen as a part of the group. Group norms are followed, and collective pressure is exerted to ensure the Process of Group effectiveness of the group.

Question No: 26

Which method of teaching encourages the maximum use of knowledge?  

Options:

  • 1.

    Problem Solution Method

  • 2.

    Laboratory Method

  • 3.

    Self-Study Method

  • 4.

    Team-Study Method

Solution:

  • The correct answer is: a. Problem Solution MethodThe Problem Solution Method encourages students to actively engage with the material by identifying problems, analyzing them, and then finding solutions. This method promotes critical thinking, problem-solving, and the application of knowledge, making it one of the most effective approaches for encouraging the maximum use of knowledge. It requires students to apply what they've learned to real-world scenarios, which deepens understanding and enhances retention of information.

    Brief overview of the other options:

    • Laboratory Method: While this method allows students to apply theoretical knowledge in practical, experimental settings, it focuses more on hands-on experience and may not engage as broadly with the entire scope of knowledge.
    • Self-Study Method: This method emphasizes independent learning, but it may not necessarily encourage the application of knowledge in dynamic problem-solving contexts as effectively as the Problem Solution Method.
    • Team-Study Method: Team study encourages collaboration and sharing of knowledge among peers, but it is not necessarily the most effective at encouraging the maximum application of knowledge through problem-solving in the same way that the Problem Solution Method does.

    Thus, the Problem Solution Method is the teaching method that best encourages the maximum use of knowledge.
