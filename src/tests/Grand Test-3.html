Question No: 2

Blood in urine:

Options:

  • 1. Hemosiderosis
  • 2. Hemopoiesis
  • 3. Hematuria
  • 4. None

Solution:

    • Hematuria is the medical term for the presence of blood in the urine.
    • This condition can be caused by various factors, including urinary tract infections, kidney stones, trauma, or underlying medical conditions affecting the urinary system.
    • Hematuria can be classified as either microscopic (blood is visible only under a microscope) or macroscopic (blood is visible to the naked eye).
    • Hemosiderosis: Hemosiderosis refers to the accumulation of iron in tissues, often due to conditions like hemochromatosis. It does not relate to the presence of blood in urine.
    • Hemopoiesis: Hemopoiesis (or hematopoiesis) is the process of blood cell formation in the bone marrow. It is unrelated to the presence of blood in urine.
    • None: This is not correct because hematuria is the proper term for blood in urine, so “None” is not a valid answer.

Question No: 3

Which of the following diseases is known as “Barometer of social welfare”?

Options:

  • 1. Leprosy
  • 2. Tuberculosis
  • 3. Cholera
  • 4. Malaria

Solution:

  • Tuberculosis (TB) is often referred to as the “Barometer of social welfare” because its prevalence is strongly associated with social and economic conditions, such as poverty, overcrowding, malnutrition, and inadequate healthcare.
    • Leprosy is an infectious disease, but it is not commonly referred to as the “Barometer of social welfare.” It was historically linked to social stigma but is not as widely used as a social welfare indicator in the same way as tuberculosis.
    • Cholera is an infectious disease associated with poor sanitation and access to clean water, but it is not specifically known as the “Barometer of social welfare.” It tends to emerge in situations of inadequate water treatment and sanitation, but TB remains a broader indicator.
    • Malaria is a vector-borne disease linked to environmental factors, such as mosquito breeding areas, rather than social welfare. While malaria control can reflect public health progress, it is not commonly described as the “Barometer of social welfare.”

Question No: 4

A female client who received general anesthesia returns from surgery. Postoperatively, which nursing diagnosis takes highest priority for this client?

Options:

  • 1. Acute pain related to surgery
  • 2. Deficient fluid volume related to blood and fluid loss from surgery
  • 3. Impaired physical mobility related to surgery
  • 4. Risk of aspiration related to anesthesia

Solution:

    • Risk of aspiration is the highest priority nursing diagnosis for a client who has received general anesthesia.
    • Anesthesia suppresses the gag and cough reflexes, increasing the risk of aspiration, especially if gastric contents are present.
    • Aspiration can lead to severe complications, such as aspiration pneumonia, which can be life-threatening. Ensuring the airway is protected and clear, takes precedence in the immediate postoperative period.

    Incorrect Options:

    • Acute pain related to surgery: Pain management is important but does not take precedence over maintaining airway patency and preventing aspiration. Pain is addressed once the client’s airway and breathing are stabilized.
    • Deficient fluid volume related to blood and fluid loss from surgery: Fluid volume deficit is a valid concern postoperatively, but it is not an immediate life-threatening condition compared to the risk of aspiration and respiratory compromise.
    • Impaired physical mobility related to surgery: Impaired mobility is a common postoperative issue, but it does not pose an immediate threat to the client’s life, unlike aspiration.

Question No: 5

A sudden redness of the skin is known as:

Options:

  • 1. Flush
  • 2. Cyanosis
  • 3. Jaundice
  • 4. Pallor

Solution:

  • A flush refers to a sudden redness of the skin, typically due to increased blood flow or dilation of blood vessels. This can occur due to various reasons such as physical exertion, emotional response, or exposure to heat.
    • Cyanosis refers to a bluish or purplish discoloration of the skin, often caused by low oxygen levels in the blood. It is the opposite of a flush and is a sign of poor circulation or respiratory distress.
    • Jaundice is the yellowing of the skin and the whites of the eyes, usually caused by an excess of bilirubin in the blood, often related to liver disease or hemolysis.
    • Pallor refers to an abnormal paleness of the skin, often due to reduced blood flow or a lack of red blood cells, as seen in conditions like anemia or shock.

  • 2. High-Fowler’s position

The most common method of reproduction of bacteria is:

  • 2. Budding
  • 3. Sexual
  • 4. Spore formation
