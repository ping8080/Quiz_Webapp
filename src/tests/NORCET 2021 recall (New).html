Question No: 9

Loud and harsh lung sound is known as:

Options:

  • 1. Stridor
  • 2. Wheezing
  • 3. Rhonchi
  • 4. Snoring

Solution:

    • Stridor is a high-pitched sound originating from the upper airway and occurring on inspiration. It is distinguished from other sounds by its intensity in the neck more so than the chest, timing (inspiratory) and pitch (high). Like wheezes, stridor is produced by airway narrowing, but only in the upper airways
    • Rhonchi (a low-pitched breath sound) crackles (a high-pitched breath sound) wheezing (a high-pitched whistling sound caused by narrowing of the bronchial tubes) stridor (a harsh, vibratory sound caused by narrowing of the upper airway).
    • Wheeze: These are the high-pitched sounds generated when the air is passing through the severely narrowed or obstructed airways in the lungs. Auscultated during both inspiration and expiration; louder during expiration.
    • Rhonchi: Also called sonorous wheeze. It is low pitched and loud enough to be heard over all the lobes of the lungs, but usually heard over the bronchi and trachea. It is generated due to any muscular spasm, large amounts of mucus inside the lungs, and may get cleared during coughing.
