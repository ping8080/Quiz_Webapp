Question No: 1

A nurse receives various antenatal mother in the OPD. Which of the following client the nurse should prioritize to be seen by the Obstetrician? (Antenatal care during pregnancy-III trimester, Antenatal care, Midwifery/Obstetrics and Gynecological Nursing)

Options:

  • 1. A pregnant client at 9 weeks of gestation experiencing fatigue and reports of nausea and vomiting
  • 2. A pregnant client at 14 weeks of gestation reporting urinary frequency and lower pelvic pain
  • 3. A pregnant client at 22 weeks of gestation reporting increase in fetal movement
  • 4. A pregnant client at 34 weeks of gestation reporting nausea and constant epigastric pain

Solution:

    • A pregnant client experiencing constant epigastric pain at 34 weeks of gestation is suggestive of pre-eclampsia. The clients with pre-eclampsia complaints of a new-onset headache (with no history of headaches or migraines) that is unresponsive to medication. The client may or may not be accompanied by additional complaints of visual disturbance. Moreover, the clients also endorse right upper quadrant or epigastric pain with associated nausea or vomiting. Therefore, this client is priority as she requires immediate attention and intervention. Hence, option d. is correct.
    • Option a: Experiencing fatigue, nausea, and vomiting is a normal finding during first trimester of pregnancy.
    • Option b: Urinary frequency is a normal finding during pregnancy, however, the evidence of lower pelvic pain should be assessed but this client should not be prioritized by the nurse to be seen by the obstetrician.
    • Option c: Fetal movement increases as the pregnancy progress. Thus, it is a normal finding in this case.

Question No: 2

When prioritizing care, the nurse should implement which action first? (Diabetes Mellitus and DKA, Endocrine disorders, Adult health nursing)

Options:

  • 1. Evaluate lab report of a client on potassium sparing diuretic
  • 2. Administer sliding scale insulin to a client with a blood glucose 350 mg/dL
  • 3. Report an Hb of 12.5 g/dL of a female client to the physician
  • 4. Admitting a client scheduled for an elective hip replacement surgery

Solution:

    • The nurse should prioritize giving attention and providing care to a client with a blood glucose of 350 mg/dL. The client has a high blood glucose level and if left untreated then may lead to Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA). Therefore, care of this client should be prioritized by the nurse. Hence, option b. is correct.
    • Option a: A client on diuretic therapy should be evaluated for blood values. However, this is not the priority and hence, option a. is incorrect.
    • Option c: The Hb of 12.5 g/dL is a normal finding and thus does not needs to be prioritized.
    • Option d: Admitting a client scheduled for an elective hip replacement surgery is not a serious condition that needs prioritizing care.

Question No: 3

Which of the following is a priority intervention for a client experiencing anaphylaxis? (Anaphylaxis, Nursing Care of Patients with Common Signs and Symptoms and Management, Adult health nursing)

Options:

  • 1. Administering epinephrine
  • 2. Assessing vital signs
  • 3. Administering oxygen
  • 4. Calling for assistance

Solution:

    • Anaphylaxis is an acute systemic reaction with symptoms of an immediate-type allergic reaction. Epinephrine is the first choice of treatment in the case of anaphylaxis as it helps to relieve bronchoconstriction, increase cardiac output, and reduce vascular permeability. It works by activation of α- and β-adrenergic receptors reducing vascular permeability, bronchodilatation, edema, and positive inotropy in the heart. When administered intravenously, it has the fastest onset of action. Hence, option a. is correct.
    • Other options are appropriate but should follow the immediate administration of epinephrine.

Question No: 4

A nurse is caring for a patient with a tracheostomy tube. Which of the following interventions should the nurse prioritize when caring for this patient? (Tracheostomy care, Respiratory disorders, Adult health nursing)

Options:

  • 1. Assessing lung sounds hourly
  • 2. Providing oral care 4 hourly
  • 3. Suctioning the tracheostomy tube whenever required
  • 4. Administering oxygen via nasal cannula

Solution:

    • The priority nursing intervention when caring for a client on tracheostomy tube is suctioning the tube as needed. It maintains the airway patent by removing secretions that may obstruct the airway. Hence, option c. is correct.
    • Options a and b are appropriate interventions but ensuring a clear airway is the priority in this case.
    • Option d. is incorrect as administering O2 via nasal cannula is not required for a patient with tracheostomy.

Question No: 5

A nurse receives a client in the ER experiencing an acute asthma attack. Which of the following is the priority nursing intervention for this client? (Asthma, Respiratory disorders, Adult health nursing)

Options:

  • 1. Administer oxygen via nasal cannula
  • 2. Administer a bronchodilator inhaler
  • 3. Administer a corticosteroid inhaler
  • 4. Administer intravenous fluids

Solution:

    • The priority nursing intervention in this case would be to administer a bronchodilator inhaler (such as Albuterol and Ipratropium) as it can quickly relax the airways and facilitate an improved breathing. Hence, option b. is correct.
    • Administering corticosteroids is also appropriate but it helps in relieving inflammation.
    • Supplemental oxygen can be applied to maintain oxygen saturation above 90 to 92%. However, this does not precede bronchodilator administration.
    • IV fluids can be administered but is not the priority in the case of an acute asthma attack.

Question No: 6

A nurse is reviewing the ABG analysis of a client admitted in the unit, diagnosed with pulmonary embolism. The ABG findings shows pH 7.46, Pco2 30 mmHg, HCO3-26, and PaO2 62. What should be the priority intervention by the nurse for this client?

Options:

  • 1.

    Make the client breathe deeply and rapidly

  • 2.

    Administer supplemental O2

  • 3.

    Administer sodium bicarbonate

  • 4.

    Administer IV fluids

Solution:

    • The nurse should administer O2 to this client as the ABG analysis shows respiratory alkalosis with hypoxemia. This occurs due to hyperventilation and impaired gas exchange in patients with pulmonary embolism. Therefore, the nurse should address the issue by providing supplemental oxygen to the client. Hence, option b. is correct.
    • Option a: This is an inappropriate intervention as the client is already in hyperventilation. Encouraging the client to take deep and rapid breaths may worsen the condition.
    • Option c: This option is also not appropriate as Na2CO3 is used in the cases of metabolic acidosis rather than respiratory alkalosis.
    • Option d: This is not the priority intervention as administering IV fluids will not address the issue of hypoxemia.

(Acid-base imbalance, Nursing Care of Patients with Common Signs and Symptoms and Management, Adult health nursing)

  • 2.

    Document findings as normal

  • 3.

    Demonstrate diaphragmatic breathing exercises

  • 4.

    Administer Albuterol inhaler

Solution:

    • The ABG result shows compensated respiratory acidosis with significant hypoxemia as pH is normal but Pco2 and HCO3 are elevated. In such case, the nurse should consider initiating O2 therapy in order to address hypoxemia. Hence, option a. is correct.
    • Option b is incorrect as ABG analysis shows abnormal findings (PaO2 of 46 mmHg indicating hypoxemia).
    • Option c shows an appropriate intervention but cannot be the priority in this case. Diaphragmatic breathing is helpful in the long-term management of COPD.
    • Option d is also incorrect as a short acting beta-2 agonist is used for bronchodilation in acute bronchospasm. In this case, hypoxemia needs to be addressed and thus O2 therapy is required.

     

Question No: 8

A patient complains about regurgitation of food after dinner. Which of the following interventions should the nurse take first to avoid regurgitation of food? (Oral nutrition and feeding, nutritional needs, nursing foundations)

Options:

  • 1. Assist the patient into semi-fowler’s position
  • 2. Keep the patient NPO
  • 3. Instruct the patient to avoid milk products
  • 4. Perform suctioning

Solution:

    • The nurse should consider assisting the patient into semi-fowler’s position to avoid regurgitation of food. This position helps to reduce the risk of regurgitation by promoting the natural flow of gastric contents and reducing the risk of aspiration. Hence, option a. is correct.
    • Option b: Keeping the patient NPO can be appropriate in a few situations such as before surgery. However, in this case it would not be the priority intervention. When the patient is actively eating, positioning would help in preventing regurgitation rather than keeping the patient NPO.
    • Option c: This is also an appropriate option as avoiding certain food can help in managing acid reflux. However, this is not the first intervention in an acute situation.
    • Option d: Suctioning is not a routine intervention to prevent regurgitation.

Question No: 9

The client is admitted to the medical unit diagnosed with a pulmonary embolus. Which of the following intervention should the nurse implement first? (Pulmonary embolism, other respiratory disorders, respiratory disorders, adult health nursing)

Options:

  • 1. Administer oral anticoagulants
  • 2. Assess the client’s bowel sounds
  • 3. Prepare the client for a thoracentesis
  • 4. Provide bed rest

Solution:

    • Pulmonary embolism is a life-threatening condition which occurs when a blood clot becomes lodged in an artery in the lung and blocks blood flow to the lung. In this case, bed rest is crucial to prevent movement of clot and reduce the risk of complications such as worsening of the embolism. Hence, option d. is correct.
    • Option a: Administering anticoagulants is also essential, however initial therapy is started via IV or SC route rather than the orally.
    • Option b: Assessing the bowel sounds is appropriate but not a critical intervention in pulmonary embolism.
    • Option c: Thoracentesis helps in removing the extra fluid from the pleural space. This procedure is carried out in the case of pleural effusion rather than pulmonary embolism.

Question No: 10

Which one of the following is the correct sequence of steps of chest tube removal? (Care of chest drainage, Oxygenation needs, Nursing Foundations)

  1. Valsalva maneuver
  2. Administration of analgesic
  3. Chest X-ray
  4. Air tight dressing of the site

Options:

  • 1. 3 → 1 → 4 → 2
  • 2. 2 → 1 → 4 → 3
  • 3. 3 → 4 → 1 → 2
  • 4. 2 → 4 → 1 → 3

Solution:

  • The correct sequence of chest removal is as follows:
    • Pre-medicate the patient for pain control.
    • Remove dressings.
    • Identify anchoring sutures.
    • Instruct the patient to take a deep breath and hold it.
    • Gently and firmly withdraw the chest tube in a single motion.
    • Apply direct pressure to the site with a dressing for at least two minutes or until bleeding/drainage have subsided.
    • Perform air tight dressing of the site.
    • Properly dispose of the chest tube catheter and other used materials.
    • Obtain an upright portable chest X-ray.Hence, option b. is correct.

Question No: 12

The nurse prepares to place a nasogastric tube in an elderly male patient with a bowel obstruction. Arrange the following steps in the order the nurse should perform them. (Enteral nutrition and feeding, Nutritional needs, Nursing Foundations)

  1. Position the patient in high fowler’s position.
  2. Lubricate the tip of the tube with water-soluble lubricating jelly.
  3. Instruct the patient to swallow as the tube is advanced.
  4. Measure the length of the tube from the tip of the nose to the earlobe to the xiphoid process.
  5. Fix the tube with tape.
  6. Insert the tube through the nares and apply gentle downward pressure.

Options:

  • 1. 1 → 4 → 2 → 6 → 3 → 5
  • 2. 2 → 1 → 4 → 3 → 5 → 6
  • 3. 3 → 4 → 1 → 2 → 6 → 5
  • 4. 2 → 4 → 1 → 3 → 5 → 6

Solution:

  • The NG tube should be inserted in the following order:
    • The nurse should first place the patient in a high fowler’s position. Then the nurse should wear non-sterile gloves and measure the length of the tube from the tip of the nose to the earlobe to the xiphoid process.
    • The next step involves lubricating the tip of the tube. Now, tube should be inserted through the nares, directed parallel to the floor, towards the back of the patient’s throat.
    • Then, the nurse can ask the patient to swallow or give a cup of water with a straw to sip from to help ease the passage of the tube. The tube should be advanced with firm, constant pressure while the patient sips. At last, the nurse should anchor the tube with tape. Hence, option a. is correct.

Question No: 13

A nurse is preparing to suction a conscious client who has a tracheostomy tube and gathers the supplies needed for the procedure. Arrange the actions in order of priority that the nurse takes to perform this procedure. (Tracheostomy care, Respiratory disorders, Adult health nursing)

  1. Hyperoxygenate the client
  2. Place the client in a semi fowler’s position.
  3. Turn on the suction device and set the regulator.
  4. Apply gloves and attach the suction tubing to the suction catheter.
  5. Apply intermittent suction and slowly withdraw the catheter while rotating it back and forth.
  6. Insert the catheter into the tracheostomy until resistance is met and then pull back 1 cm.

Options:

  • 1. 2, 3, 4, 1, 6, 5
  • 2. 1, 4, 6, 3, 2, 5
  • 3. 4, 5, 2, 6, 1, 3
  • 4. 6, 4, 5, 2, 1, 3

Solution:

  • Suctioning of the tracheostomy tube is also performed to remove mucus and to maintain a patent airway. The priority actions for tracheostomy suction are as follows:
    • The nurse prepares the patient by explaining the procedure and providing adequate sedation and pain relief as needed.
    • The patient is placed in semi fowler’s position if conscious or in a lateral position facing the healthcare provider if unconscious.
    • Then the suction device is turned on and adjust suction pressure gauge between 80 – 120 mmHg.
    • Then the nurse should hyperoxygenate the client before suctioning.
    • Then suctioning is performed by inserting the catheter into the tracheostomy until resistance is met.
    • Then intermittent suction is applied and slowly the catheter is removed while rotating it back and forth.
    • Each suction should not be longer than 5-10 seconds.
    • After completing suctioning, the outcomes from the procedure should be evaluated and documented. Hence, option a. is correct.

Question No: 14

A mountaineer’s feet suddenly become pale and starts to feel very cold. After 30 min. of assessment, the affected part becomes prickly and numb. Place the following intervention in the correct sequence for a client with frostbite. (Frost bite, First aid, Nursing foundations)

  1. Administer pain medication
  2. Apply loose, non-adhesive dressing
  3. Monitor for compartment syndrome
  4. Immerse the feet in warm water (40-420C)

Options:

  • 1. 4, 1, 2, 3
  • 2. 1, 2, 3, 4
  • 3. 3, 4, 2, 1
  • 4. 2, 3, 4, 1

Solution:

    • Frost bite is cold-related disease (ischemia) that occurs after exposure to freezing temperatures. The sequence of interventions for the management of frost bite is as follows:
    • The mainstay of frostbite management is rapid rewarming in water (40–42°C) until the affected skin appears red/purple.
    • Then pain relief medication should be administered as rewarming can be painful.
    • After that, the area should be protected with a dressing (non-adhesive, dry, thick, loose dressing) to prevent further injury and infection.
    • At the end, the healthcare provider should look for signs of compartment syndrome, which can develop due to swelling and restricted blood flow after rewarming.

  • 4. Place the client in an upright position

A nurse is caring for a ventilator patient diagnosed with ARDS admitted in the medical ICU. The high-pressure alarm on the ventilator goes off. On assessment, the nurse observes SpO2 as 87% and the patient is struggling to sit up. Which action should be taken by the nurse on priority? (Acute respiratory distress syndrome, respiratory disorders, adult health nursing)

Options:

  • 1. Reassure the patient that the ventilator will breathe for him.
  • 2. Manually ventilate the patient while assessing possible reasons for the high-pressure alarm.
  • 3. Increase the FiO2 on ventilator to 100%.
  • 4. Insert an oral airway.

Solution:

    • The nurse should consider performing manual ventilation as this will allow to deliver a FiO2 of 100%. Meanwhile the nurse can determine the cause of the high-pressure alarm. Hence, option b. is correct.
    • Option a: The patient may require some kind of reassurance but this intervention is not priority.
    • Option c: Increasing the excessive volume, pressure, or flow on the ventilator may cause lung damage, stomach insufflation or hemodynamic compromise in a patient with ARDS.
    • Option d: The patient may require an oral airway to prevent the patient from biting on the endotracheal tube. However, the first step should be the resolution of hypoxemia and finding the cause of high pressure alarm.

Question No: 17

The nurse is providing discharge instructions to a patient diagnosed with asthma. Place the steps in the correct order that the nurse should teach the patient about the use of an MDI (Meter-dose inhaler): (Asthma, Respiratory disorders, Adult health nursing)

  1. Hold the breath for at least 10 seconds
  2. Wait for at least 1 minute between puffs
  3. Open the mouth and place the mouthpiece 1 to 2 inches away
  4. Press down firmly on the canister and breathe deeply through the mouth
  5. Tilt the head back and breathe out fully
  6. Remove the inhaler cap and shake the inhaler

Options:

  • 1. 6, 5, 3, 4, 1, 2
  • 2. 1, 5, 6, 4, 2, 3
  • 3. 5, 6, 4, 3, 2, 1
  • 4. 4, 3, 2, 1, 6, 5

Solution:

  • The steps of using an MDI are as follows:
    • Remove the inhaler cap from the MDI.
    • Shake the MDI for 5 seconds.
    • Position the inhaler so the top of the canister faces up.
    • Put the finger pointer on the top of the canister. Support the bottom of the MDI with your thumb.
    • Breathe out normally and as fully as you can, away from the MDI.
    • Either place the MDI between the teeth and close the lips tightly around the mouthpiece or hold it 1–2 inches (2.5–5 cm) away from the mouth.
    • Press the canister down with the finger to release the medicine. Inhale deeply and slowly through the mouth until your lungs are filled. Do not breathe in through the nose. Inhaling should take 4–6 seconds.
    • Hold the breath at least for 5–10 seconds to allow the medicine to get into the small airways of the lungs.
    • Remove the inhaler from the mouth. Turn the head and breathe out normally.
    • Wait about 1 minute between puffs.
    • Now, put the cap on the MDI.

  • 4. Get the help of the nursing assistant to stay with the client meanwhile call the physician

Question No: 19

Which is the priority nursing intervention when providing post-procedure care to a client who had a cardiac catheterization via a brachial artery in the first hour after the procedure? (Invasive cardiac procedures, Cardiovascular system, Adult Health nursing)

Options:

  • 1. Monitor vital signs every 15 minutes.
  • 2. Maintain the client in the supine position.
  • 3. Keep the client’s lower extremities in extension.
  • 4. Administer the prescribed oxygen at 4 l/min via nasal cannula.

Solution:

    • The nurse should consider monitoring vital signs of the client after cardiac catheterization to detect early signs of complications such as bleeding, hematoma formation, or signs showing hemodynamic instability. The nurse should consider assessing vital signs including HR, RR, BP, and SpO2. Hence, option a. is correct.
    • Option b: Maintaining the client in the supine position would be an appropriate intervention when cardiac catheterization is performed via the femoral artery. This position helps in preventing strain on the access site and reduces the risk of bleeding.
    • Option c: Keeping the client’s lower extremities in extension would be an appropriate intervention when cardiac catheterization would have been performed via the femoral artery. Extending the leg in such cases is helpful in preventing complications at the insertion site.
    • Option d: Administering oxygen is not required after cardiac catheterization unless the patient is desaturating or has other respiratory concerns.

Question No: 20

What is the priority nursing intervention in the immediate post-operative period for a client who has undergone amputation of left leg? (Care of surgical patients, Introduction, Adult health nursing)

Options:

  • 1. Administering analgesics as prescribed
  • 2. Assessing surgical site
  • 3. Applying a compression bandage to the residual limb
  • 4. Providing emotional support and counselling

Solution:

    • Assessing the surgical site is crucial for a client who has undergone amputation. This intervention helps to monitor for any signs of bleeding, infection, or hematoma formation. The nurse should ensure that the incision site is stable so that life-threatening complications (such as haemorrhage) can be prevented. Hence, option b. is correct.
    • Option a: Administering analgesics to manage post-operative pain is also essential, however, ensuring the stability of the surgical site should be the nurse’s priority in this case.
    • Option c: Compression bandage are applied to shape the residual limb and control swelling. However, this intervention is done when the incision site is stable and free from complications.
    • Option d: Providing emotional support is also crucial but it is an intervention planned for a long-term goal. In the immediate post-operative period, physical stability is the priority including monitoring for complications of the surgical site.
