Question No: 1

Which endocrine gland is situated in the lower part of neck on the thyroid cartilage? 

Options:

  • 1.

    Pituitary gland

  • 2.

    Adrenal gland

  • 3.

    Thyroid gland

  • 4.

    Pineal gland

Solution:

    • The thyroid gland is situated in the lower part of the neck, anterior to the trachea, and on the thyroid cartilage.
    • It consists of two lobes connected by an isthmus, positioned on either side of the trachea.
    • It is responsible for producing thyroid hormones (T3 and T4) and calcitonin, which regulate metabolism and calcium levels.

    Incorrect answer:

    Option a. Pituitary gland:

    • The pituitary gland is located in the brain within the sella turcica of the sphenoid bone, not in the neck.
    • It is the master endocrine gland that regulates other glands but does not correspond to the anatomical description in the question.

    Option b. Adrenal gland:

    • The adrenal glands (also known as suprarenal glands) are situated on top of the kidneys, in the retroperitoneal space.
    • The adrenal glands have two parts: the cortex and the medulla.
    • The cortex is the outer part of the gland. It produces the hormones cortisol and aldosterone. The medulla, meanwhile, is the inner part of the gland. It produces the hormones adrenaline and noradrenaline.

    Option d. Pineal gland:

    • The pineal gland is a small pea-shaped gland located in the epithalamus, near the center of the brain.
    • Regulates sleep-wake cycles and circadian rhythms by producing and releasing melatonin in response to darkness.

    Reference: Prabhjot Kaur, textbook of anatomy and physiology, lotus publishers, 2nd edition, P 372-373

Options:

  • 1.

    Edema

  • 2.

    Muscle spasms

  • 3.

    Kussmaul breathing

  • 4.

    Abdominal distention

Solution:

    • Hypokalemia refers to a low potassium level in the blood. One of the common clinical findings associated with hypokalemia is abdominal distention, which occurs due to decreased gastrointestinal motility caused by impaired smooth muscle function. Potassium plays a crucial role in muscle contractions, and its deficiency can lead to reduced peristalsis, resulting in abdominal distention.

    Explanation for Incorrect Options:

    • Option a. Edema: Edema is not typically associated with hypokalemia. It is more commonly linked to conditions like fluid overload, heart failure, or hypoalbuminemia.
    • Option b. Muscle spasms: Muscle spasms are more indicative of hypocalcemia or hypomagnesemia, not hypokalemia. In hypokalemia, muscle weakness or cramping is a more common symptom.
    • Option c. Kussmaul breathing: Kussmaul breathing (deep and labored breathing) is a compensatory mechanism associated with metabolic acidosis, such as in diabetic ketoacidosis, and is not a typical finding of hypokalemia.

Question No: 24

Which effect may lead to toxic reactions when a drug is taken continuously or repeatedly?

Options:

  • 1.

    Refractoriness

  • 2.

    Cumulative effect

  • 3.

    Tolerance

  • 4.

    Tachyphylaxis

Solution:

    • The cumulative effect occurs when a drug is taken continuously or repeatedly and accumulates in the body due to insufficient elimination.
    • This can lead to toxic reactions because the body is unable to metabolize or excrete the drug at a rate that matches its administration. The drug’s effects become stronger over time, which can lead to toxicity.

    Explanation for Incorrect Options:

    • Option a. Refractoriness: Refractoriness refers to the reduced response to a drug after it has been used repeatedly or continuously, but it does not directly cause toxic reactions. It is more related to a decreased therapeutic effect.
    • Option c. Tolerance:
      • Tolerance is a phenomenon where the body becomes less responsive to the effects of a drug over time.
      • As tolerance develops, higher doses of the drug may be required to achieve the same effect, but this does not directly cause toxic reactions.
      • However, it may lead to increased doses, which can then increase the risk of toxicity.
    • Option d. Tachyphylaxis: Tachyphylaxis is the rapid onset of tolerance to a drug, where the response to the drug diminishes quickly after repeated doses. It does not necessarily lead to toxic reactions, but rather to a loss of effectiveness after initial exposure.

Question No: 98

An energy consuming process by which cells of the human body absorb molecules (such as proteins) by engulfing them is called _______________.

Options:

  • 1.

    Lipid diffusion

  • 2.

    Aqueous diffusion

  • 3.

    Exocytosis 

  • 4.

    Endocytosis

Solution:

    • Endocytosis is an energy-consuming process where cells absorb molecules (such as proteins, lipids, or other large molecules) by engulfing them within a vesicle.
    • The cell membrane wraps around the molecules, forming a vesicle that is brought into the cell.
    • This process is vital for the uptake of large molecules that cannot pass through the cell membrane via simple diffusion.

    Rationale for Incorrect Answers:

    • Lipid diffusion: Lipid diffusion refers to the movement of lipid molecules across the cell membrane. It is a passive process that does not require energy and is not related to the engulfment of large molecules.
    • Aqueous diffusion: Aqueous diffusion is the process by which water and soluble substances move across membranes. Like lipid diffusion, this is a passive process and does not involve energy consumption or the engulfment of molecules.
    • Exocytosis: Exocytosis is the opposite of endocytosis. It is the process by which cells expel materials, such as waste or secretory products, by vesicles fusing with the cell membrane. It is not related to the intake of molecules.

    Reference: Kozier and Erb, Fundamental of Nursing, II Edition 2018, p. 1454
