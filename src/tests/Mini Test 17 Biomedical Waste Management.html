Question No: 9

Which of the following is not a high-heat system for the treatment of biomedical waste?

Options:

  • 1.

    Hydroclaving

     

  • 2.

    Incineration

     

  • 3.

    Microwaving

     

  • 4.

    Plasma pyrolysis  

Solution:

    • Microwaving is a low-heat system for the treatment of biomedical waste.
    • The heating systems used for the treatment and disposal of biomedical waste are:

    Low-Heat System

    High-Heat System

    Autoclaving: Uses steam. It is of two types:

    1. Gravity flow autoclave

    2. Vacuum autoclave

     

    Microwaving: Uses moist heat and steam generated by microwave energy

     

    ·         Incineration: Waste is destroyed/denatured under high temperatures.  

    ·         Plasma pyrolysis: Waste is treated at high temperatures under controlled conditions to form gases that are subjected to combustion in the secondary chamber.

    ·         Hydroclaving: The waste is subjected to indirect heating by applying steam to the outer jacket.

    ·         Dry heat sterilization: Waste sharps are treated using dry heat at a temperature >185 ºC for 150 minutes. 

Question No: 10

A pregnant woman at 18 weeks of gestation with bleeding per vagina had a miscarriage. The products of conception are sent for appropriate tests. Which of the following is not a disposal option for the same?

Options:

  • 1.

    Incineration

     

  • 2.

    Plasma pyrolysis     

     

  • 3.

    Deep burial  

     

  • 4.

    Encapsulation   

Solution:

    • Encapsulation is not a disposal option for human anatomical waste. It is a disposal technique used for expired and discarded medicines along with chemical wastes.
    • Human anatomical waste such as an aborted fetus is put in a yellow bag and is disposed of by either incineration, plasma pyrolysis, or deep burial.
    • The biomedical waste management rule is applicable to all those who collect, receive, store, dispose and treat biomedical waste. The wastes are categorised and segregated at the point of generation of waste. A color coding system with 4 colors namely yellow, red, white, and blue is used for waste segregation.
    • The segregated waste is then disposed of by various methods. These are:
      • Incineration - This is a dry oxidation process done using high temperatures. The types are rotary kiln, pyrolytic incinerator, and single chamber incinerator.
      • Chemical disinfection - Chemicals are added to waste to kill or inactivate the microorganisms. Mostly used for treating liquid wastes.
      • Wet and dry thermal treatment - Shredded infectious waste is exposed to high temperature, high-pressure steam and it resembles an autoclave sterilization process.
      • Microwave irradiation - Microorganisms are killed using high-frequency microwaves. Its efficiency should be checked routinely.
      • Land disposal - Either municipal disposal sites or sanitary landfills are used.
      • Inertization - Waste is mixed with cement and other substances before disposal to reduce toxicity.
    • Disposal by deep burial is permitted only in rural areas which lack common bio-medical treatment facilities. Permission from the concerned authority is needed and guidelines issued by the central pollution control board should be followed.

Question No: 14

Identify the correct sequence of doffing the PPE kit.

  1. Remove outer gloves and perform hand hygiene.
  2. Remove shoe covers only by touching the other surface and perform hand hygiene.
  3. Remove eyepiece by holding the straps and perform hand hygiene.
  4. Remove gown slowly by holding the gown at the waist and pulling, without touching the outer surface.
  5. Remove inner gloves and perform hand hygiene.  

Options:

  • 1.

    1, 2, 3, 4, 5

     

  • 2.

    2, 1, 3, 4, 5

     

  • 3.

    1, 2, 4, 3, 5

     

  • 4.

    2, 3, 1, 4, 5  

Solution:

    • The correct sequence of doffing (removing) the PPE kit is 2, 1, 3, 4, 5.

    Preparation for doffing the PPE:  

    • To be performed only in the designated area
    • Check for any leak or soiling
    • The doffing room should have two chairs, dirty and clean
    • All the PPE must be discarded in the red bin
    • Hand hygiene must be performed after every step

    Steps of doffing the PPE:

    • Engage the observer and ask him to check the PPE - observer should check for any leak, tear, or contamination of PPE. If contaminated, then it should be disinfected with alcohol-based wipes.
    • Disinfect the gloves.
    • Remove and discard shoe covers.
    • Disinfect and remove the outer pair of gloves.
    • Disinfect inner gloves.
    • Remove face shield/eye goggle - loosen the screw and remove the face shield by holding it from the back handle. Discard it.
    • Remove hood and cover all (gown)
      • Holding the suit at the arms, slide it off the shoulder slightly, then pull the suit from the side of the waist.
      • Roll the arms out one by one.
    • Disinfect and remove inner gloves.
    • Perform hand hygiene and wear a new pair of gloves.
    • Remove and discard N-95 mask.
    • Disinfect gloves.
    • Sit on the clean chair and disinfect shoes using alcohol wipes.
    • Disinfect and remove gloves.
    • Perform hand hygiene.
    • Wipe the shoes on the sodium hypochlorite soaked doormat at the exit.
    • Exit the doffing area and take a shower.
