Which of the following is correct regarding umbilical cord?

  • 1.

    Umbilical vein carries deoxygenated blood from placenta to the fetus. 

  • 2.

    Umbilical cord has Wharton's jelly

  • 3.

    Umbilical artery carries oxygenated blood from fetus to the placenta

  • 4.

    Umbilical cord has 2 veins and 1 artery

Solution:

  • Umbilical cord

    • The umbilical cord is pearl-white, 1–2 cm in diameter, 50–60 cm long, eccentrically positioned, and contains the left and right umbilical arteries, left umbilical vein, and mucus connective tissue (Wharton jelly).
    • Highly oxygenated and nutrient-enriched blood returns to the fetus from the placenta via the left umbilical vein. (Note: Highly oxygenated blood is carried by the left umbilical vein, not by an artery.)
    • Poorly oxygenated and nutrient-poor fetal blood returns to the placenta via right and left umbilical arteries.

    • Contact precautions
    • Airborne precautions
    • Droplet precautions
    • Contact precautions are used for patients with known or suspected infection representing an increased risk for contact transmission. These precautions involve the following components:
    • Ensuring appropriate patient placement such as in isolation rooms.
    • Using PPE appropriately, including gloves and gown.
    • Limiting the transport and movement of patients outside the room.
    • Using disposable or patient specific care equipment.
    • Prioritizing cleaning and disinfection of the rooms of patients on contact precautions.

     

    • Droplet precautions are used for patients with known or suspected infection which can be transmitted by respiratory droplets of the infected patient. These precautions involve the following components:

     

    • Putting a mask on the patient in order to control the source of infection.
    • Ensuring appropriate patient placement in a single room if possible. When single room is not available, utilize the recommendations for alternative patient placement considerations in the guideline for isolation precautions.
    • Use personal protective equipment appropriately such as donning mask upon entry into the patient room.
    • Limiting transport and movement of patients outside the room.

     

    • Airborne precautions are used for patients with known or suspected infection transmitted by the airborne route. These precautions involve the following components:
    • Putting a mask on the patient in order to control the source of infection.
    • Ensuring appropriate patient placement in an airborne infection isolation room as per isolation precautions.
    • Restricting susceptible healthcare professionals from entering the room of patients known or suspected to have infections.
    • Using PPE appropriately, including N95 or higher level respirator for healthcare professionals.
    • Limiting transport of patients outside the room, however, if transport or movement is necessary, then patient should be asked to wear a surgical mask.
    • Immunizing susceptible persons following unprotected contact with vaccine-preventable infections.
